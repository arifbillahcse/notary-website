What Is an Apostille?

Foreign entities often require the certification of notarized documents. This process means one or more additional certificates are added, which confirms the validity of the notary's commission for use overseas.

An Apostille is an official government certification that authenticates the origin of a public document so it can be recognized in another country that is a member of the Hague Convention. It removes the need for lengthy chain-authentication processes, making your documents internationally valid quickly and simply.

The United States joined the Hague Apostille Convention in 1981. Over 120 countries worldwide recognize apostilled documents.
